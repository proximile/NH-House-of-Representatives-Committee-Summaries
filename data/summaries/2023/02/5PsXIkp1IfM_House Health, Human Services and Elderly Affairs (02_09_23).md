### **Comprehensive Summary of NH House Health, Human Services, and Elderly Affairs Meeting (02/09/23)**

The New Hampshire House Committee on Health, Human Services, and Elderly Affairs convened on February 9, 2023, to discuss and vote on several bills related to healthcare, pain management, and the state’s therapeutic cannabis program. The meeting focused on improving patient access to care, addressing the opioid crisis, and expanding the state’s therapeutic cannabis program. Below is a cohesive summary of the key discussions and outcomes:

---

### **1. Physical Therapy and Pain Management**
**House Bill 188 (HB 188)**  
- **Purpose**: Removes the requirement that patients must see a physician within 25 days if there is no documented improvement in their condition while receiving physical therapy.  
- **Support**: Prime sponsor **Representative Popovich Mueller** and physical therapists argued that the rule is outdated, citing a primary care physician shortage, barriers for uninsured patients, and the expertise of physical therapists to determine when a doctor’s visit is necessary
